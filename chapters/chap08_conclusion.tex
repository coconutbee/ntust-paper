\label{sec:conclusion}
We set out to study \emph{posture-requested} personalized text-to-image
generation---a single reference identity, an explicit textual posture
request, and accountability for realizing both---a regime where adapter-based
personalizers, bound by their backbone's pose prior, either fail to rotate or
lose identity. The thesis's central finding is that this failure is not a
missing capability but a \emph{resource allocation} problem: FLUX.2-klein's
frozen VAE and joint attention already carry and use identity content
(Sec.~\ref{sec:refverify}), but identity and pose are forced to compete for
one shared, softmax-normalized attention budget (Sec.~\ref{sec:mechanism}),
and every training-free heuristic that improves identity---reference
duplication, block ordering---turns out to be the same operation on that
budget in disguise. Every other contribution follows from treating this as
the object to engineer, not work around. On the \emph{method} side, an
in-context SAM-3D skeleton control supplies the rotation, and because
duplication and ordering are coarse, discrete ways of re-weighting the
budget, we distilled them into a $\sim$200-parameter learned attention-budget
allocator that refines identity further---the first trained component in
this line of work to improve over the strongest training-free configuration
at no pose cost. On the \emph{measurement} side, the same insight motivated
asking whether an aggregate identity score can be trusted at all once pose
varies: our \emph{pose-rectified performance metrics}---an explainable pose
check, a pose-adapted identity read-out (PAEs), and a pose-aware identity
that credits the face cosine only at the posture actually produced---expose
the profile-view identity loss the task is defined by and prevent frontal
collapse from masquerading as identity preservation. Method and metric are
thus two views of one finding: once identity is read at the pose actually
produced, and once the backbone's attention budget is allocated rather than
left to a fixed heuristic, posture-requested personalization stops being a
trade-off and becomes a controllable point on one. Future work includes
scaling the learned allocator to many more identities via a self-distilled
data engine, testing it on a non-distilled backbone, and closing the
chin-down pitch axis with pose-targeted data or 3D-aware control---each a
natural next step once the attention budget, rather than the symptom, is the
thing being engineered.
