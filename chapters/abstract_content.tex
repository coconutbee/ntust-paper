Recent identity-preserving personalization methods faithfully reproduce a reference face~\cite{ipadapter,photomaker,instantid,pulid,uniportrait}, but remain strongly biased toward near-frontal generations: under \emph{posture-requested} instructions---turning to the side, looking over the shoulder, tilting the chin up or down---existing methods often collapse back to a frontal view. We study \emph{posture-requested personalized text-to-image generation}: given a \emph{single} reference identity and a textual posture request, generate that identity at the requested posture, and measure the result with metrics that are not themselves biased toward frontal faces.

We present \textbf{Rotate-ID}, a \emph{one-shot} system built on a few-step rectified-flow DiT (FLUX.2-klein). An in-context structural pose control drives the requested head turn; identity is preserved by re-allocating the frozen backbone's attention toward the reference---a mechanism we verify causally and distill into a $\sim$200-parameter learned gate (the $\gamma$-gate) that refines identity \emph{at no pose cost}.

Face encoders read identity reliably near frontal but \emph{under}-report it as the same person turns away---so merely staying frontal scores high identity---and CLIP alignment cannot tell whether the posture was realized. Our \emph{pose-rectified performance metrics} close both gaps: an explainable 3D pose check verifies each turn, a pose-adapted read-out scores identity with yaw-matched recognizers, and a \emph{pose-aware} score credits identity only at the posture actually produced, instantiated as a benchmark of $40$ posture requests over eight pose categories.

Under these metrics, Rotate-ID more than doubles the yaw accuracy of PhotoMaker-V2, the strongest identity-personalization baseline ($82.6\%$ vs.\ $36.1\%$), realizes over-the-shoulder rotations at measured yaw angles no baseline attains, and remains the strongest tuning-free method under the pose-aware identity once the frontal-collapsing baselines' apparent identity lead disappears.
